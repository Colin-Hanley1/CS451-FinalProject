§ 1 · The Question Motivation
Given only collegiate production and high school recruiting data,, how much of a prospect's early career NBA production we predict using ML techniques?

NBA teams spend millions on scouting that combines stats, video, and human judgment. This project sets out to solve a narrower question, how much of a player's early career production can be predicted using only collegiate box-score information?

§ 4 · Feature Engineering 13 new columns beyond the raw 28

Feature engineering to account for positional differnces and shot volume

  • 01 Position-relative z-scores for 7 stats. A guard with 4 RPG is average; a center with 4 RPG is below replacement.
  • 02 Volume-weighted 3P% — shrinkage toward the 31.2% league mean by attempt rate.
  • 03 Def. versatility — SPG × BPG.
  • 04 Recruit × efficiency — did the player live up to the hype?
  • 05 Build score — position-relative height + weight.

§ 11 · Technical Architecture How a CSV becomes a dashboard

One pipeline, four layers, one command.

I · Data
Raw inputs
fixeddata.csv — 1,042 × 28 (scraped Basketball-Reference + 247Sports) · 2026class.csv — 40 × 27 (incoming class).
▼
II · Preprocess
Shared feature engineering
src/preprocess.py — 13 engineered columns, position z-scores, shrinkage 3P%, build score. Same function called on historical and 2026 data for distributional parity.
▼
III · Train
Model fitting + explanation + clustering
src/train.py — temporal split, 3 regressors + 2 classifiers with RandomizedSearchCV, SHAP TreeExplainer, per-position K-means, global PCA. One command, ~2 min on a laptop.

IV · Artifacts
Serialised for the frontend
models/*.pkl (7 files) · site/data/*.json (5 files) · figures/*.png (12 plots). bigboard.json regenerated separately via generate_bigboard.py without retraining.

Static single-page dashboard
site/index.html — vanilla HTML/CSS/JS, no framework. Ridge coefficients + scaler in model_meta.json drive the in-browser live predictor; SHAP/comps are read from prospects.json. Total deploy bundle: ~700 KB.
§ 12 · Limitations & Next Steps What the model doesn't see

Four-fifths of the story lives outside the box score.

  • R² 20% explained bounds the value of purely statistical scouting. The rest is strength of schedule, physical testing, shot selection, tape.
  • N Only 120 centers — K-means uses k=2 for that group; the predictor is noisiest on bigs.
  • POS “Forward” is a noisy label — it conflates 3&D wings with interior fours. Position-relative z-scores help but don't fix it.
  • ERA No era adjustment — a 2003 draftee played a very different game. The model is agnostic to when you were drafted.
  • TODO Next — SOS adjustment, combine data, per-game shot-chart features, an ensemble with scouting-consensus priors.
